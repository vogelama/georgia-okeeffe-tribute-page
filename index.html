Georgia O’Keeffe

Mother of American Modernism

Her distinct flowers, dramatic cityscapes, glowing landscapes, and depictions of bones against the stark desert sky are iconic and original contributions to American Modernism.

- Georgia O'Keeffe Museum

A timeline of Georgia O'Keeffe's life:

  • 1887 - Georgia Totto O’Keeffe was born.
  • 1902 - By the time O’Keeffe reached the age of 16 years, she had received 5 years of art training at various schools.
  • 1905 - O’Keeffe graduated high school in 1905 and moved to live with an aunt in Chicago so she could study at the Art Institute of Chicago.
  • 1907 - O’Keeffe, however was stricken with Typhoid and did not return to the Art Institute of Chicago in 1907. Instead she moved to New York to study with the Art Student League.
  • 1908 - In 1908, O’Keeffe won the League’s William Merritt Chase still-life prize for her oil painting Untitled (Dead Rabbit with Copper Pot).
  • 1912 - From 1912 to 1914, O’Keeffe worked as a teacher of drawing and penmanship in the public schools in Amarillo, Texas. During this time O’Keeffe met Arthur Wesley Dow who introduced her to the idea that art is the expression of the artist’s personal ideas and feelings.
  • 1914 - O’Keeffe began teaching art at Columbia University during the fall of 1915. During this time, O’Keeffe began to experiment with Dow’s theories.
  • 1915 - By this time, O’Keeffe was teaching art at the West Texas State Normal College in Canyon, Texas. During her time in Texas, O’Keeffe abandoned charcoal and began to use watercolor in her paintings.
  • 1924 - O'Keeffe marries Stieglitz. During this time O’Keeffe painted New York cityscapes and the view of Lake George.
  • 1928 - O'Keeffe spends the summer in New Mexico. That summer forever changed the life of Georgia O’Keeffe.
  • 1946 - From 1923 until his death in 1946, Stieglitz worked tirelessly to arrange annual shows for O’Keeffe.
  • 1951 - O’Keeffe was again feeling wanderlust and began a decade of worldwide travel.
  • 1971 - O'Keeffe's eyesight starts to fail.
  • 1977 - O’Keeffe received the Medal of Freedom from President Ford.
  • 1986 - She died in Santa Fe in 1986. Georgia O’Keeffe was 98 years old.
